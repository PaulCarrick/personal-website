Where Paul Lives

Paul lives in what is considered as one of the most beautiful spots in North America. He lives in Eastsound Washington state on Orcas Island. Orcas Island is pronounced as "OR-kus" Island, with emphasis on the first syllable. The "Orcas" part sounds like "OR-kuss," and it’s named after the viceroy of New Spain, Juan Vicente de Güemes, who held the title of "Conde de Orcasitas." Orcas Island is located approximately 15 miles west of Bellingham and 70 miles north-west of Seattle. The only way to get to the island is via boat or plane. Most islanders use the Washington State Ferries to get on and off the island. The island is full natural wonders: eagles, owls, hawks, herons, deer, otters, orcas (yes, the whales in this case), seals, sea lions, and more. Paul's wife proudly proclaims that the island has no predators on it (although we did have a bear for a few days until it got "deported").
