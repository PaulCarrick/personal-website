Where Paul Carrick Lives

Paul lives in what is considered as one of the most beautiful spots in North America. He lives in Eastsound Washington state on Orcas Island. Orcas Island is pronounced as "OR-kus" Island, with emphasis on the first syllable. The "Orcas" part sounds like "OR-kuss," and it’s named after the viceroy of New Spain, Juan Vicente de Güemes, who held the title of "Conde de Orcasitas." Orcas Island is located approximately 15 miles west of Bellingham and 70 miles north-west of Seattle. The only way to get to the island is via boat or plane. Most islanders use the Washington State Ferries to get on and off the island. The island is full natural wonders: eagles, owls, hawks, herons, deer, otters, orcas (yes, the whales in this case), seals, sea lions, and more. Paul's wife proudly proclaims that the island has no predators on it (although we did have a bear for a few days until it got "deported").

Paul's Home

Lakes and Water Falls

Orcas Island has several lakes: Cascade Lake, Mountain Lake, Twin Lakes, and Killabrew Lake amount others. Paul lives next to Moran State Park and is only about 1/2 mile from Cascade Lake. In the summer Paul loves to swim in the lake.

Wildlife

Orcas Island has eagles.

Orcas also have herons, which you would think would be a good thing, but they like the fish in Paul's pond..

Orcas has deer which locals call "horned cockroaches" because they are so prevelant and they destroy our gardens. You can tell the tourists because they will stop in the middle of the road to watch the deer.

Orcas also has seals.

Orcas has both river and sea otters. Paul had a wonderful experience with them once. As mentioned, Paul likes to swim in Cascade Lake in the summer. At the end of one swim Paul was coming back while swimming. As he got near the end of the swim he saw some heads in the water. At first he thought they were children. As he got closer he thought "thouse dont look right". He then realized he was swimming with otters.
