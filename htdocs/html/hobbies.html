Orcas A Capella

Paul and his wife (Virginia) sang with the Orcas A cappella Singers. The term a cappella is Italian and translates literally to "in the style of the chapel." It originally referred to singing without instrumental accompaniment, as was commonly done in church chapels during the Renaissance period. Photography

Paul has enjoyed photography since he was in high school (where he was in photojournalism). He actually worked for a local paper as a photo journalist out of High School. He developed his own black and white, color, and slide photographs back in the days of film. Today he just does digital. Almost all the photographs on this site were taken by him except for those he is in.

This is an award-winning photograph that Paul took. He is proud of it. It won third place in a contest at CalArts. The reason he is proud is that there were professional photographers that worked for studios that competed in the competition. It has an interesting story behind it. The original photo was taken as a slide (Kodachrome). The subject suggested Paul enter it in the contest. The contest only took paper photographs so Paul decided to do a transfer to paper. This involves making a paper copy that is a negative, The paper copy is then exposed to another copy which gives a normal image. In the process there was a slight error in the color of the paper negative which gave a slight pinkish color. Additionally, there was an error during exposure that caused a slight softening of the image. The judges liked the color and softness which is why Paul won 3rd place.

Home Automation

Paul has been doing home automation since the days of X-10 (the 1980s) when you would push the button and hope that something happened. Much has changed since them with the advent of Z-Wave, IoT, cameras, motion sensors, Alexa, Google, and Apple. His house is fully automated. He can access it anywhere in the world to set the thermostat, turn lights on or off, check the cameras, see if the laundry is done or the refrigerator door has been left open. Many of the lights are sensor based so he doesn't need to turn the lights on or off.
